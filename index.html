Background
My passion for games stretches back to my childhood. I remember the endless hours playing Paper Mario and Tony Hawk Pro Skater with my siblings on the Nintendo 64, and nowadays, game nights are what bring my friends and me together. I want to recreate this for others, thus I am determined to develop games that foster memories and relationships.

I began making video games in high school. I picked up GameMaker Studio and made my very first platformer, and after that, I was hooked. In college, I was fortunate enough to be introduced to graphics programming and virtual reality development in Unity. Programming courses at UW-Madison were largely taught with Java but with a focus on theory, so my knowledge of data structures and algorithms extends beyond a single language.
